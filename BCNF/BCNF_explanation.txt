BCNF Stage Explanation — Group 9
================================

PREREQUISITE
------------
This stage builds directly on the 3NF design. All 3NF tables are carried
forward unchanged EXCEPT the Equipment tables (see below).


WHAT IS BCNF?
-------------
For every functional dependency X → Y, X must be a candidate key.
BCNF is stricter than 3NF.


BCNF VIOLATION FOUND IN 3NF
---------------------------
Table affected: Equipment

3NF design (carried from 2NF):
  Equipment(EquipmentUsed, EquipmentRentalCost)
  FD claimed: EquipmentUsed → EquipmentRentalCost

Problem:
  This table is in 3NF (no transitive dependencies), but it violates BCNF
  because the functional dependency is not valid in the real data.
  The same equipment type has different rental costs on different projects:
    Crane:  5000 (P001), 5500 (P002), 6000 (P004), 6500 (P007)
    Mixer:   800 (P003), 1000 (P005),  900 (P006)

  EquipmentUsed alone cannot determine EquipmentRentalCost.
  The correct determinant is the full composite key:
    (ProjectID, EquipmentID) → RentalCost

BCNF decomposition (change from 3NF):
  BEFORE (3NF):
    Equipment(EquipmentUsed, EquipmentRentalCost)
    Project_Equipment(ProjectID, EquipmentUsed)

  AFTER (BCNF):
    Equipment(EquipmentID, EquipmentName)
    Project_Equipment(ProjectID, EquipmentID, RentalCost)


FUNCTIONAL DEPENDENCIES CHECKED — ALL OTHER TABLES
---------------------------------------------------

Clients
  PK: ClientID
  FD: ClientID → ClientName, ClientPhone, ClientEmail, ClientCity
  Determinant is candidate key. BCNF satisfied.

Supervisors
  PK: SupervisorID
  FD: SupervisorID → SupervisorName, SupervisorPhone
  BCNF satisfied.

Projects
  PK: ProjectID
  FD: ProjectID → all other columns
  ClientID and SupervisorID are foreign keys only.
  BCNF satisfied.

Workers
  PK: WorkerID
  FD: WorkerID → WorkerName, WorkerPhone, WorkerHourlyRate
  WorkerName is also a candidate key (unique).
  BCNF satisfied.

WorkerSkills
  PK: (WorkerID, Skill) — no non-key attributes. BCNF satisfied.

WorkerCertifications
  PK: (WorkerID, Certification) — no non-key attributes. BCNF satisfied.

Project_Worker
  PK: (ProjectID, WorkerID) — junction only. BCNF satisfied.

Suppliers
  PK: SupplierID
  FD: SupplierID → SupplierName, SupplierCity
  BCNF satisfied.

SupplierPhones
  PK: (SupplierID, PhoneNumber) — BCNF satisfied.

SupplierMaterial
  PK: (SupplierID, Material)
  FD: (SupplierID, Material) → UnitCost
  Full composite key is the determinant. BCNF satisfied.

Project_Supplier_Material
  PK: (ProjectID, SupplierID, Material) — junction only. BCNF satisfied.

WHAT CHANGED FROM 3NF TO BCNF
------------------------------
Only the Equipment tables changed:

  3NF Equipment.csv          →  BCNF Equipment.csv
  (EquipmentUsed, Cost)          (EquipmentID, EquipmentName)

  3NF Project_Equipment.csv  →  BCNF Project_Equipment.csv
  (ProjectID, EquipmentUsed)   (ProjectID, EquipmentID, RentalCost)

All other 11 tables are identical between 3NF and BCNF.


WHY THE DESIGN IS NOW IN BCNF
------------------------------
Every functional dependency has a determinant that is a candidate key.
The Equipment decomposition ensures RentalCost is determined by
(ProjectID, EquipmentID), not by equipment type alone.
